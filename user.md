# UX design process

## Define the user and their goal

- Who are they? 
  A customer collecting loyalty rewards.
  A clerk giving rewards and history tracking. 
- What do they want to achieve?
  The customer wants to collect loyalty rewards and claim rewards on free purchases.
  The clerk wants to manage rewards claims and keep track of customers claims and loyalty points.
- How tech-savvy are they?
  They just know how use a browser.

## Identify the entry point
Website is the entry point for both.
For the customer the entry point is a receipt with site url and entry code to collect loyalty points. For first time customer it is going to require sign up and for returning customer its going to require sign in.
For the clerk the entry point it's a clerk log in where they enter credentials and allows them to access a dashboard.

## Map out the steps 
- What's the very first action the user takes?
  The customer is going enter the receipt code into the web app. 
  The clerk enters their credentials to access dashboard.
- What choices do they face along the way?
  The customer has the choice to register/log in to collect points and claim rewards.
  The clerk has the choice to reward customers claims, track customers actions via history.

## Include decision points 
- Customers
  Collect points, keeps track of their loyalty points collections.
  Gets notified when they can claim a reward, giving them a reward choice.
  Chooses a reward, waits for approval and collect.
- Clerk
  Creates rewards and claim requirements (loyalty points a customer has to collect before making a claim). 
  Manages pending claims and returns the reward to the customer.

## Determine the endpoint
- Customers 
  Collecting approved rewards and lands on success screen.
- Clerk 
  Dashboard with claims history.
